Industry Readiness Program

Touched on in the presentation, the Industry Readiness Program is one of the best parts about being in Minnovate

The Minnovate Industry Readiness Program seeks to equip students with the skills most important and common to engineering fields, recognizing the importance of both technical and non-technical. Here's how it works:

  • Held over the course of the spring semester.
  • Groups meet once a week for 2 hours in the Anderson Student Innovation Lab.
  • Students are grouped into teams and have the entire semester to design a prototype.
  • Students receive certification from Anderson Labs on their transcripts.
  • Students build technical, teamwork, and communication skills in a hands-on project
  • Program culminates with a final presentation in front of head of Design at Anderson Labs and other program members!

    FAQ

    • The industry readiness program is an immersion into what the professional engineering process will look like. Meeting every week in the Anderson Innovation Lab, we will pair students into groups and each team will work to develop a prototype over the course of the semester.

    • As a member of Minnovate, you will be able to work on projects ranging from CAD and 3D Printing, to Arduinos and Sensors. You will learn about the engineering process and refine your soft and networking skills at the same time, giving you a leg up come interview time!

    • Joining Minnovate will help prepare you for the Innovation Challenge, a concurrent event that happens each semester. Each iteration of the Innovation Challenge focuses on three or four new prompts in which students will create a prototype for. Past years have had focus in Healthcare, App Development, Industrial Engineering, and Sustainability.

    • While the Industry Readiness Program occurs during the Spring, our Fall program can consist of Networking events, Brainstorming sessions, Case Studies in conjuction with NOBE, and Mock Interviews. We are committed to preparing you for a successful career beyond college!
